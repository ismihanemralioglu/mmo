## Project Overview

In this study, the three-dimensional (3D) structure of a functional domain of the BRCA1 protein was predicted using an *ab initio* structure prediction approach. BRCA1 is a key tumor suppressor involved in DNA damage repair, genome stability, and cell cycle regulation. Due to its large size and partially unresolved experimental structure, computational modeling provides important insights into its functional domains.

---

## Methodology

The structural prediction was carried out using Google DeepMind AlphaFold 3.

- The selected amino acid sequence corresponded to the BRCA1 RING finger domain (UniProt: P38398).
- The sequence was submitted as a monomeric protein input.
- Five alternative structural models were generated by the algorithm.
- **model_2** was chosen as the reference structure based on the highest confidence scores and structural consistency.
- Confidence metrics such as **pLDDT scores**, **pTM score**, and **PAE (Predicted Aligned Error)** matrices were analyzed for structural validation.

---

## Structural Analysis and Visualization

The predicted `.cif` structure was visualized using PyMOL with per-residue confidence mapping:

- **Blue (pLDDT > 90):** Highly stable β-sheet and α-helix core regions  
- **Cyan/Yellow (90 > pLDDT > 50):** Moderately flexible loop regions  
- **Orange (pLDDT < 50):** Disordered terminal segments with low prediction confidence  

### Functional Domain Highlight

In the PyMOL visualization (`brca1_pymol_render.png`), zinc-coordinating cysteine and histidine residues within the RING domain were highlighted as green spheres, emphasizing their role in protein–protein interaction and ubiquitin ligase activity.

## Conclusion

The AlphaFold-based *ab initio* modeling successfully produced a structurally consistent model of the BRCA1 RING domain. The results highlight a well-defined zinc-binding core region supported by flexible peripheral loops.

This computational approach demonstrates the utility of AI-driven protein structure prediction in studying functionally important but experimentally challenging protein domains.
